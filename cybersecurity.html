Cyber Security Guidelines

Protecting Your Digital Identity and Data

Understanding Cyber Security

Cybersecurity refers to the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These cyberattacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information; extorting money from users; or interrupting normal business processes.

Why It Matters

In today's interconnected world, everyone benefits from advanced cyber defense programs. At an individual level, a cybersecurity attack can result in everything from identity theft to extortion attempts, to the loss of important data like family photos.

Potential Cyber Security Hazards

Phishing Attacks

Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity. Attackers may send emails that appear to be from legitimate organizations asking for your login credentials, financial information, or personal data.

Identity Theft

When someone steals your personal information to commit fraud. This can include using your name, Aadhaar number, bank account details, or other identifying information without your permission.

Malware & Ransomware

Malicious software designed to harm or exploit any programmable device or network. Ransomware specifically locks your data and demands payment for its release.

Unsecured Networks

Using public Wi-Fi networks without proper security measures can expose your data to interception by cybercriminals.

Social Engineering

Psychological manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential information. This can happen through phone calls, messages, or in-person interactions.

Essential Cyber Security Practices

Password Security

  • Use strong, unique passwords for different accounts
  • Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible
  • Never share passwords via email or text messages
  • Use a reputable password manager

Network Security

  • Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions
  • Use a VPN when accessing public networks
  • Keep your router firmware updated
  • Change default router passwords

Email & Communication Safety

  • Verify sender addresses before clicking links
  • Don't open attachments from unknown sources
  • Be cautious of urgent or threatening language in emails
  • Check for spelling and grammar errors in suspicious emails

Device Security

  • Keep operating systems and apps updated
  • Install reputable antivirus software
  • Enable device encryption
  • Regularly back up important data

Cyber Security Laws in India

India has established legal frameworks to address cyber crimes and protect digital citizens:

Information Technology Act, 2000

The primary law dealing with cybercrime and electronic commerce in India. Key sections include:

  • Section 43: Penalty for damage to computer, computer system
  • Section 66: Computer related offenses
  • Section 66C: Identity theft
  • Section 66D: Cheating by personation using computer resource
  • Section 67: Publishing obscene information electronically

Legal Consequences

Cyber crimes in India can lead to:

  • Imprisonment ranging from 3 years to life depending on the severity
  • Fines ranging from ₹1 lakh to ₹10 crore
  • Both imprisonment and fines in many cases
  • Civil liability for damages caused

Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In)

CERT-In is the national nodal agency for responding to computer security incidents. If you're a victim of cyber crime, you should:

  • Report to your local police station or cyber crime cell
  • File a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in
  • Contact CERT-In for technical assistance
  • Preserve all evidence related to the incident
